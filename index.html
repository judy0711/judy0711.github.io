Research Interests

Keyword: Multimodal AI (Video Understanding, Vision Language Models), Brain Inspired AI

My interest in research began at the intersection of human visual perception and computer vision. I was particularly fascinated by the differences in how humans and AI perceive the world. To better understand how the human brain works, I delved into the field of psychology with a focus on visual perception. This exploration deepened my understanding of human cognition and strengthened my motivation to develop AI systems that are more aligned with how humans see and interpret the environment.

Over time, this curiosity evolved into interest in multimodal AI. I am especially drawn to the challenge of building models that can perceive, generate, and reason across multiple modalities in ways that are both efficient and safe. Ultimately, I hope to contribute to the development of AI models that can collaborate and coexist with humans in the real-world environment.

In the long term, my goal is to contribute to the development of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), with a particular focus on grounding it in insights from human cognition. I believe that bridging human perceptual and cognitive processes with AI development is essential to creating systems that are truly capable of understanding and adapting to complex human contexts.
