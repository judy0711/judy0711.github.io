Playerâ€™s Prefrontal Cortex Activation Through fNIRS : Near-Misses and Stop Buttons in Slot Machine
Data Analysis in Cognitive Science Research Class Group Paper, 2023

This study examined how the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) and dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (dlPFC) respond to win, near-miss, and loss scenarios in a within-subject design. Findings revealed heightened OFC activity during near-misses and increased dlPFC activation when participants had control (via a stop button), suggesting that near-misses engage reward systems and perceived control enhances cognitive processing.
